UI/UX DESIGN

ELEEMO CASE STUDY

#PRODUCTDESIGN #UI/UXDESIGN #GRAPHICDESIGN #CASESTUDY #MOBILEDESIGN

SUMMARY:

People struggle knowing which causes to trust and donate to and they need one concise app to guide the way. Eleemo helps you find and contribute to causes that you care about. We provide transparency and verification for charities so you can trust in knowing exactly where your support is going.

SCOPE:

Graphic Design, UI/UX, Research, Competitive Analysis, User Research, Testing, User Interviews

Tools: Figma, Miro, Google Forms, Zoom, Trello, Slack

ROLE:

Designing, Planning,User Research,Wireframes, Prototyping, and Testing.
Full Team: In collaboration with members Sarrah Ang-Bejar and John Hand

PROBLEM:

Compassionate donors need an easy way to find and donate to charitable causes without having to spend a lot of time researching their legitimacy and transparency.

SOLUTION:

Eleemo allows users to find charities that match their interests and values while providing transparency and legitimacy for charities so that users can feel confident and trust in knowing exactly how their donations will be used.

PROCESS

STEP ONE:

Survey Analysis and Insights: Researching the reputation of charities is the biggest challenge donors face. Most people donate directly through the organization’s website.

1. Researching the reputation of charities is one of the biggest challenge donors face.

5. Most people we surveyed donate two or more times a year.
6. Most of the people we surveyed were between the ages of 30-45

STEP TWO:

Conducting One on One User Interviews: We received more information and better answers by speaking with people directly. Our surveys were a juming off point to ask more targetted questions.

We carried out 3 interiews and also talked with a local charity. This helped us get a better idea of our user persona and gather data about pain points, what is important, and what our users would want.

USER RESEARCH

STEP ONE:

Affinity Diagram: We intervied 6 people in one on one virtual meetings and then categorized their statements below into larger themes in order to have a better idea and grasp of their needs and desires when donating.

User Research Anaysis: It became clear that the biggest pains for our participants was spending a lot of time and energy researching legitimacy and transparency of charities.

The more time they spend with this process, and the more difficult the website is to use, the less confident and comfortable users feel donating.

STEP TWO:

User Persona: Julia is a Sales Manager in Alpharetta, GA. She is introverted, but enjoys feeling like she is part of a larger cause and regularly donates to local charities. The goals she would like to accomplish include:

1. Finding charitable causes that align with her interests and values
2. Verifying their legitimacy
3. Understanding clearly how donations will be allocated
4. Donating painlessly
5. Receiving a confirmation and feeling connected to the charities success

DEFINITION AND IDEATION

STEP ONE:

Problem Statement:

We have observed... that a lack of transparency, limited ability to find charities that align with user’s values and interests, and obscured legitimacy in the donation process cause compassionate donors to become frustrated, spend more time researching, and diminishes trust.

How might we...design an app to make it easier to find interest-based causes, improve transparency and verify legitimacy so that donors are successful based on an increase in the number of recipient needs met per month, increased frequency of an individual’s donations, and a net promoter score over 7.

STEP TWO:

User Insight: Julia Cortez is a compassionate donor who wants to have an impact on her local community and needs a convenient way to find transparent, authentic charities that match her interests and values.

She wants to contribute to meaningful causes but spending a lot of  time researching is inconvenient, increases frustration, and reduces  her confidence.

User Scenario: Julia wants to find charities that align with her interests and values. She wants to make sure they are legitimate and have a clear understanding of how donations will be allocated. Doing the research on her own is daunting, so she uses eleemo to achieve her goals quickly and easily. She receives a confirmation email with a tax receipt and follow-up emails sharing the success of the campaigns she contributed to. She knows her donations helped make a difference in the receipients’ lives and she feels connected to a larger cause that has made an impact she would not be able to on her own.

STEP THREE:

User Journey Map: We decided to create a custom, circular User Journey Map to capture the cyclical nature of the donation process. It starts with frustrated users discovering eleemo and creating a personalized list of charities, finding verification badges, trancparency pie charts and links to organization websites and social media accounts and donating through our app. They then receive a confirmation email with a tax receipt and social sharing links. Finally, before the tax year comes to a close they get a reminder email with a link to all of their tax receipts conveniently stored in one place where they can download them all with one click. This touch point re-engages users and reminds them about the app while their pockets are lined with Christmas bonuses.

STEP FOUR:

Brainstorming and Feature Prioritization Matrix: We brainstormed feature ideas using the “I Like, I Wish, What If” method, then voted on our favorites. We then created a Feature Prioritization Matrix to determine which features were the highest priority and most feesable. Because we had so many good ideas that would be easy to implement, we voted on the features that most directly addressed our problem statement.

TESTING AND ITERATION

STEP ONE:

We asked users to:
1. Setup a credit card and select interests
2. Make a donation

Based on feedback we got there we iterated the donation button and removed the interest selection feature.  We also made charity reccomendations on the confirmation page.

Feedback we got includes:
-Streamline onboarding and tutorial process.
-Create confirmation page after adding payment methods to verify success.
-Title pie charts for more context
-Include specific causes for charities so users aren’t just donating to the charity itself.
-Streamlining the donation process and iterate on the CTA button.
-Clarifying the Interest Selection feature and it’s flow

STEP TWO:

Our main changes in the user flow: between iterations our changes involved flesing out the onboarding / training process, as well as removing the Interest Selection feature from settings.

Based on feedback from user testing we used smooth animation to define our app name, expain what it’s for and how to use it, and create a home screen that encouraged donation through graphics and a call to action.

The interest selection feature was removed because 1. It complicated the onboarding user flow, 2. Users already have two other ways to find charities they like, and 3. The favorites and recommendations based on past donations already offer a personalized experience.

CONCLUSIONS

STEP ONE:

Conclusions Include:
1. We learned how better to use our tools.
2. We learned that most people find donations through church or Facebook.
3. We learned that most people donate directly through organization websites.
4. We learned that transparency is the single most important thing to donors.
5. We learned that legitimacy is also highly important.
6. We learned about the charity verification process.
7. We learned the strengths and weaknesses of competitor apps.

STEP TWO:

In future iterations we might include features such as:
- An interest checklist with recommended charities based on selections
- A cherity wish registry where users could mark multiple charities and share the list with friends
- The ability to share contributions on social media and invite friends to join in support
- A map of local charities and the ability to donate physical goods via drop-off or volunteer labor
- Monthly subscription donations
- Sponsorship agreements for charities who wish to be featured
- Goal trackers for charities trying to reach a specific number of donations or dollar amount
- Searchable tags that of which charities could have multiple
- Reward programs for gamification and incentives
